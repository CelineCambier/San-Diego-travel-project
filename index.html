Why San Diego?

San Diego, the Amercia's Finest City

Back in the early 1970s the slogan "America's Finest City" was coined for San Diego as part of advertising drive to attract visitors to the city. The nickname has stuck ever since, it's our slogan. With origins in the rejection of our small city, it became a rallying cry to celebrate who we are, boost our civic pride, and recognize our potential.

This SoCal metropolis offers more than just temperate year-round weather. There are plenty of pristine beaches, a vibrant arts scene, a diversity of cultures and a rich military history that lend San Diego its distinct charm

The best places to visit

Balboa Park

Balboa Park is a cultural oasis that includes 17 museums, gardens galore and the world-famous San Diego Zoo.

Address:

1549 El Prado

What I like about it

The park has a rich history reflected through its stunning architecture, thought-provoking exhibits installations, and cultural events throughout the year.

Gaslamp quarter

This area is rich with cultural offerings that include theatres, art galleries, symphony halls, concert venues and museums.

Address:

199 Fifth Avenue

What I like about it

It is a mix of historic buildings and high-tech skyscrapers, packed with restaurants and bars that range from flashy nightclubs to low-key dives and everything in-between.

La Jolla Cove

La Jolla Cove is a small cove with a beach that is surrounded by cliffs. California sea lions can be found in the waters.

Address:

1100 Coast Blvd

What I like about it

The Cove at La Jolla is a vacationer's dream in San Diego with deep blue water, small waves and white sand.
